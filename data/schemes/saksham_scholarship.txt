SCHEME NAME: AICTE Saksham Scholarship Scheme for Specially-Abled Students
ADMINISTERING BODY: All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), Ministry of Education
SCHEME TYPE: Central Sector Scheme

OBJECTIVE: Support students with disabilities in pursuing technical education (diploma/degree) by removing financial barriers.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA:
- Applicant must be a specially-abled student with a minimum of 40% disability, certified by a government medical board (private hospital/individual doctor certificates are NOT accepted)
- Must be admitted to the 1st year of a diploma or degree-level technical program, OR 2nd year via lateral entry, at an AICTE-approved institution
- Annual family income from all sources must not exceed Rs. 8,00,000
- Admission must be through the centralized government admission process — management quota admissions are NOT eligible
- Open to applicants of any gender

BENEFIT AMOUNT: Rs. 50,000 per year (lump sum) covering tuition fees, books, computers, software, and equipment; NOT covering hostel or medical expenses. Duration: up to 4 years for first-year admission, up to 3 years for lateral entry (some sources cite up to 3/2 years respectively — verify current guideline)

RENEWAL CONDITIONS: Must submit promotion certificate/mark sheet and institution letter annually; scholarship discontinued if student is not promoted to next year

APPLICATION: National Scholarship Portal (scholarships.gov.in) or dedicated AICTE portal, deadlines typically around October 31

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS: Disability certificate from government medical board (specifying percentage), income certificate, admission proof, bank passbook, Aadhaar

SOURCE: AICTE / National Scholarship Portal guidelines, verified June 2026
