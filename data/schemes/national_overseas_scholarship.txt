SCHEME NAME: National Overseas Scholarship (NOS) for SC, DNT, Landless Agricultural Labourers and Traditional Artisans
ADMINISTERING BODY: Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India
SCHEME TYPE: Central Sector Scheme

OBJECTIVE: Enable low-income meritorious students from marginalized communities to pursue Master's or PhD degrees at top universities abroad.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA:
- Applicant must belong to Scheduled Caste, Denotified/Nomadic/Semi-Nomadic Tribes, Landless Agricultural Labourers, or Traditional Artisans category
- Must have secured admission (or be applying for admission) to an eligible Master's or PhD program at a recognized foreign university — does NOT cover undergraduate study
- Minimum 60% marks (or equivalent grade) in the qualifying examination
- Annual family income must not exceed Rs. 8,00,000 (note: some older guidelines cite Rs. 6,00,000 — verify current notification)
- Applicant must be under 35 years of age
- Only one child per family can receive this scholarship
- Must not already hold a foreign Master's/PhD degree, and must not be receiving another scholarship for overseas study

BENEFIT AMOUNT: Covers tuition fees (paid directly to university), living/maintenance allowance (~USD 15,400/year for USA and most countries, ~GBP 9,900/year for UK), one-time airfare, book/study allowance, visa fees. Funded for up to 3 years (Master's) or 4 years (PhD)

TOTAL SLOTS: 125 scholarships annually (115 for SC, 6 for DNT, 4 for Landless Agricultural Labourers/Traditional Artisans); 30% reserved for women

APPLICATION: nosmsje.gov.in (Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment portal), typically opens February with two application rounds annually

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS: Caste certificate, income certificate, academic transcripts, admission offer letter from foreign university, passport, NOC from employer if currently employed

SOURCE: Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment guidelines, verified June 2026
